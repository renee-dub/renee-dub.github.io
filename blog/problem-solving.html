Problem Solving

A story about a time I was blocked on a simple problem.

I am currently blocked on the javascript kata gradebook getAverage function challenge. I have some code which appears to be correct, but when I run the tests I am getting error messages. To try to help myself I have spent some time googling for answers. I have found posts on discord from other students who have struggled with these as well. And I have gone over notes I have taken from previous lectures as well as re-watched previous lectures. My next step will be to google the actual error message, then reach out to my peers or a facilitator for some guidance. I always feel anxious and know that I am really struggling with something when I procrastinate and take lots of little breaks. I try to come back to my desk and make some more attempts to solve the challenge and make sure I sit back and take a break to calm myself down. I have learnt that I am very good at avoiding problems! I alreay knew this about myself but it's becoming more apparent! I know that I need to be brave and ask for help so that is what I will do. I have also learnt that I know more than I think and that I can always learn more. I try to remind myself that I am new to this and haven't mastered anything YET. But I will.

A story about a time I elegantly solved a problem.

After being stuck on the gradebook challenge to create a function to return an average, I managed to get the code right so the tests passed! I spent some time googling for potential answers. I then read through some posts on discord to see if anyone else had struggled on this challenge. I then went through my notes from previous lectures and found the code I thought would work and it did! It was such a smooth process once I was in the right headspace (cool, calm and collected), and open to being curious about how to solve the challenge.
