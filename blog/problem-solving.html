Reflection: how confident I feel using different problem-solving techniques.

  • Pseudocode:

    I understand and see the benefit of this technique, I haven't had much practice with this though, so I am going to make an effort and see how it helps.

  • Trying something:

    This is my go-to problem-solving technique. I like to try different methods and ways to solve a coding challenge, and see what happens. I find this can be a long-winded technique if you are just blindly typing bits of code and seeing what happens but I find it's a great way to get started and test what you have just learnt.

  • Rubber ducky method:

    I am confident using this technique, although it isn't my go-to. I agree that when you talk a problem through, either with others or an inanimate object, the answer sometimes just appears! This is a technique I would like to put into practice more.

  • Reading error messages:

    I am not super confident using this technique yet but I am slowly learning to understand how error messages can help and what they are actually trying to tell me. I like that in VS Code error messages let you know exactly where an issue is, it's just becoming more confident with understanding what information is being given that I need to work on.

  • Console.logging:

    This would be one of my go-to problem-solving techniques. It is nice and easy to console log a function or statement to see if it has worked as you had hoped.

  • Googling:

    My first-stop-shop is googling a problem. I like that there is a wealth of information on the world-wide-web just waiting to be discovered. I am getting more and more confident with this technique. It is sifting through the options to find an article that you understand that takes the most time. But it makes for great practice trying out the possible answers or trying to understand these to figure out if it will work for your particular problem.

  • Asking your peers for help:

    Asking my peers for help is not one of my strong points and is an area that I know I need to work on during this course. I don't have a lot of confidence in asking for help as I always think I won't be understood, or people will think I'm stupid for asking something and this really holds me back. I will work on this as I know it will be hugely beneficial and great practice for when I am in a tech role later this year!

  • Asking coaches for help:

    Again. I am not comfortable asking my coaches for help even though I know that is what they are here for and are only too happy to help! I get quite anxious as I don't want to be seen as stupid or feel like I am taking up someones time. This is another probelm-solving technique that I can see is hugely beneficial to my learning so I want to work on being brave and asking for help from the facilitators if I need it.

  • Improving your process with reflection:

    I am pretty confident with this technique as it gives you some time and space to think about how your day, or an area of learning has gone, and whether or not you need to dive deeper or ask for help. Making this a daily habit has been great and I now feel anxious if I haven't reflected on a process or learning outcome. The further into this course I go, I know that this will become even more valuable for me to solidify my learnings and identify areas where I need to focus more.
